Spatial Pattern Analysis

Discussion

This maps illustrates the temporal changes in Delhi City's urban spatial patterns from 1990 to 2020, categorizing areas into "Scattered Settlement," "Sub Urban Fringe," "Secondary Core," and "Primary Core." Over this 30-year period, Delhi exhibits a profound and widespread urban expansion. In 1990, the "Primary Core" was concentrated centrally, surrounded by relatively distinct "Secondary Core" and "Sub Urban Fringe" areas. By 2000, there's a noticeable outward growth of these core areas. This expansion becomes significantly more pronounced in 2010, with the "Primary Core" expanding substantially and merging with several "Secondary Core" regions, illustrating a rapidly densifying urban fabric. By 2020, the maps show an almost contiguous and extensive "Primary Core" dominating the landscape, with "Secondary Core" and "Sub Urban Fringe" areas pushing further outwards, indicating a massive urban sprawl and the transformation of peripheral areas into integrated parts of the highly urbanized metropolis.
